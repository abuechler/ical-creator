# Kanton Bern (BE) - Research Summary

**Research Date:** 2026-01-11
**Status:** Research Complete

## Overview

Kanton Bern uses a **centralized cantonal system** with **regional differentiation** for German-speaking and French-speaking areas. The canton is the most populous after Zurich and has the highest number of municipalities in Switzerland.

## Holiday System: Centralized with Regional Calendars

### Legal Framework

The **Bildungs- und Kulturdirektion (BKD)** sets school holidays for the entire canton according to Article 8, paragraph 3 of the Volksschulgesetz (VSG; BSG 432.210). This has been in effect since the 2010/11 school year.

### Two Regional Calendars

The canton maintains **two separate official holiday calendars**:

| Calendar | Region | System | Alignment |
|----------|--------|--------|-----------|
| **German-speaking** | Most of canton | DIN week numbering | Cantonal standard |
| **French-speaking** | Berner Jura | BEJUNE coordination | Cantons Jura, Neuchatel |

### Fixed Holidays (Cantonal Level)

For German-speaking areas, the standard holidays are:

| Holiday | Typical Weeks | Duration |
|---------|---------------|----------|
| Herbstferien (Autumn) | KW 39-41 | 3 weeks |
| Winterferien (Christmas) | KW 52/1 | 2 weeks |
| Fruhlingsferien (Spring) | KW 15-16 | 2 weeks |
| Sommerferien (Summer) | KW 28-32 | 5 weeks |

**School year:** 38-39 weeks (varies by Gemeinde)

### Flexible Elements

| Element | Flexibility | Notes |
|---------|-------------|-------|
| **Sportferien (February)** | KW 2-14 | Gemeinde chooses within range |
| **Additional week** | 38-week schools only | For Kindergarten/Primary |
| **Alpine tourism regions** | KW 15-21 for spring | Frutigen-Niedersimmental, Interlaken-Oberhasli, Obersimmental-Saanen |

### Biel/Bienne Special Case

Biel and neighboring municipalities (Evilard, Orvin, Plagne, Romont, Vauffelin) use an **alternating system**:
- **Odd start years** (2025/26, 2027/28, 2029/30): Follow French-speaking calendar
- **Even start years** (2026/27, 2028/29, 2030/31): Follow German-speaking calendar

This aligns with students attending Gymnasien in the Berner Jura.

## Language Considerations

### German-speaking Municipalities (~295)

- Verwaltungskreise: Bern-Mittelland, Emmental, Frutigen-Niedersimmental, Interlaken-Oberhasli, Oberaargau, Obersimmental-Saanen, Seeland, Thun
- Follow DIN-based cantonal calendar
- Search terms: Ferienplan, Schulferien, Ferienordnung

### French-speaking Municipalities (~38)

- Verwaltungskreis: Berner Jura (arrondissement administratif du Jura bernois)
- Aligned with Cantons Jura and Neuchatel (BEJUNE coordination)
- Search terms: Vacances scolaires, Calendrier des vacances, Plan des vacances

### Bilingual Municipalities (~6)

- Verwaltungskreis: Biel/Bienne
- Both languages accepted for administration
- May use either calendar (alternating by year)
- Largest: Biel/Bienne (55,000+ inhabitants)

### German-speaking Enclaves in Berner Jura

Some municipalities in Berner Jura are majority German-speaking:
- Seehof (91% German)
- Schelten (83% German)
- Mont-Tramelan (71% German)
- Rebevelier (67% German)

These still follow the French-speaking calendar administratively.

## Suggested Data Collection Approach

### Phase 1: Cantonal Data (30 min - 1 hour)

1. Download both official PDFs (German and French calendars)
2. Extract dates for both language regions
3. Document as base templates
4. Note exceptions (alpine tourism, Biel alternating)

### Phase 2: Verify Special Cases (1-2 hours)

1. **Alpine tourism regions**: Check if Gstaad, Interlaken, etc. use different spring holiday timing
2. **Biel/Bienne**: Confirm alternating schedule and affected municipalities
3. **Sample Sportferien variation**: Check 3-4 Gemeinden per Verwaltungskreis

### Phase 3: Full Collection (if needed)

**Scenario A: Strong cantonal uniformity (likely)**
- If variations limited to Sportferien timing only
- 2 calendars (DE/FR) + exception list
- Estimated time: 2-4 hours total

**Scenario B: Significant local variation**
- If each Gemeinde has unique schedule
- Need 334 Gemeinden
- Estimated time: 25-35 hours

### Recommended Strategy

1. **Start with cantonal PDFs** - covers majority of data
2. **Focus on exceptions** - alpine regions, Biel alternating system
3. **Sample by Verwaltungskreis** - verify Sportferien variation
4. **Priority regions**: Bern-Mittelland (largest), Berner Jura (French), Biel/Bienne (bilingual)

## Data Collection Complexity

| Factor | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| Centralized system | **Greatly reduces** effort (2 calendars cover most) |
| Bilingual canton | **Adds complexity** (must track 2 language regions) |
| Alpine tourism exceptions | **Adds complexity** (spring holiday flexibility) |
| Sportferien flexibility (KW 2-14) | **Main variable** to track per Gemeinde |
| 334 Gemeinden | **High** if full collection needed, but likely unnecessary |
| Biel alternating system | **Unique case** to document carefully |

## Estimated Effort

| Approach | Scope | Time | Confidence |
|----------|-------|------|------------|
| Cantonal only | 2 calendars | 30 min - 1 hour | Medium (misses Sportferien variation) |
| Cantonal + exceptions | 2 calendars + ~20 exception cases | 2-4 hours | High |
| Full (per Gemeinde) | 334 municipalities | 25-35 hours | Very High |

**Recommendation:** The cantonal approach with documented exceptions is sufficient for most use cases. Full per-Gemeinde collection is only needed if Sportferien timing must be known for every municipality.

## Key Differences from Other Cantons

| Aspect | Bern (BE) | Zurich (ZH) | Aargau (AG) |
|--------|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| System | Centralized | Per-Gemeinde | Hybrid |
| Calendars | 2 (DE/FR) | ~160 variations | 1 cantonal + local |
| Variation | Sportferien only | Full schedule | 4 weeks local |
| Languages | German + French | German only | German only |
| Effort level | Low-Medium | Very High | Medium |
